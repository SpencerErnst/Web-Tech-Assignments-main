CS:GO Skin Quality Classification

CS:GO skins are classified by rarity tiers that determine their frequency in case openings. From common Consumer Grade (white) items to extremely rare Covert (red) items, each tier represents a significant drop in probability.

The CS:GO skin market represents a sophisticated digital economy where virtual weapon skins can command significant real-world value. These cosmetic items vary in rarity, wear condition, pattern, and special attributes which directly influence their market price.

Case Opening Probabilities

Understanding the probability distribution is critical for proper investment analysis. As shown above, Mil-Spec (blue) items are most common at nearly 80%, while Covert (red) items appear in just 0.64% of openings. StatTrak™ variants are approximately 10x rarer than their standard counterparts.

Weapon Wear System: Float Values

Every skin in CS:GO has a "float value" - a number between 0 and 1 that determines how worn or damaged the skin appears. Lower values indicate better condition, with visual damage increasing as the float value rises.

Trade Up Contracts

Trade Up Contracts let players exchange 10 skins of identical quality for one random skin of the next highest quality tier. Understanding collection combinations and probabilities is essential for profitable trade-ups.

Case Opening Simulator

Cases are the primary source of most skins. Understanding case mechanics and the probabilities involved is crucial for investors who prefer buying skins directly rather than gambling through case openings.

The Contraband Story: M4A4 Howl

The M4A4 Howl is the only Contraband-tier skin in the game. Originally released as a Covert skin, it was discovered that the artwork was plagiarized. Valve removed it from cases and redesigned it, creating an exclusive collectible that continues to appreciate in value.
